PhyDSL Tutorial - Alien Miner

In this tutorial we are going to build a sample game called Alien Miner. In this game an alien navigates across a platform world inside of a cave full of gems and dangerous flying meteorites. The goal of the game is to capture all the gems while crossing a cave in an alien world. For every emerald and diamond collected the player will receive 20 and 30 points respectively. While emeralds are collected when the alien collides with them, diamonds are collected when the player taps them with her or his fingers. If the alien gets hit by a meteorite the player will loose 20 points. The game has a total duration of 120 seconds. However, if the player reaches the teleportation portal at the need of the level the game will come to an end.



In Alien Miner the main actor of the game i.e. The Alien, is controlled using on-screen controls. Three buttons specify the vertical and horizontal acceleration of the alien.

PhyDSL Type System

PhyDSL offers an enumeration-based type system, that allows developers to specify concrete values for a wide range of variables to be used throughout the game.
